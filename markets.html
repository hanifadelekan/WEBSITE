Trading Strategy

  • Probability based Hedging on short and long selling - vary the percentage of long or short positions taken out to hedge a trade based on probability of success e.g - buy £100 apple with 0.75 probability of price rising, hedge with short position that is equal to 0.25 of the long position. Price goes to 200, = £100 Long - £25 Short = £75 profit. Price goes to £50, = £12.50 - £50 = £37.50 loss. However, this is assuming I sell my long position, if I expect the price to go up again I could exit my short position at £12.50 profit then keep the stock. Short selling fees will need to be taken into account.
